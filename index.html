Linnea, Sorenne and Marie's

Colombia Adventure

Walled cities, island beaches and the heights of Bogota - March 2026

Fri 20 Mar - Arrival in Cartagena

After a long travel day we landed on the Caribbean coast and made our way into the walled old city of Cartagena. The warm night air, balconies dripping with bougainvillea, and horse-drawn carriages clip-clopping over the cobblestones were the perfect welcome. We found a courtyard restaurant for a late dinner - though by the time the food arrived, little Marie had already curled up across the chairs, completely worn out. A great sign of adventures to come!

Sat 21 Mar - Forts, Sculptures and a Sunset on the Walls

Our first full day was pure Cartagena. We started in Bocagrande, posing with a giant reclining bronze sculpture beneath the palm-lined high-rises, then climbed up to the mighty Castillo San Felipe de Barajas. The girls loved exploring the fort's dark stone tunnels and scrambling along the ramparts under the Colombian flag. As the heat faded we walked the old city walls (Las Murallas) and joined the crowd for a spectacular sunset over the sea. We ended the evening with drinks and dinner up on the ramparts at Cafe del Mar, watching a sliver of crescent moon rise over the sentry boxes.

Sun 22 Mar - Off to the Islands

We traded the city for the sea and headed down the Baru peninsula to Hotel Las Islas, a barefoot-luxury resort tucked into the dry tropical forest of the Rosario Islands. Wooden boardwalks wound through the trees between the cabins, and we spotted the named little villas along the way. Dinner that night was magical - candlelit tables under the canopy, rustic carved wooden cups, and the sound of the forest all around us.

Mon 23 - Wed 26 Mar - Beach Days at Las Islas

A glorious stretch of slow island days. We followed the boardwalks down to the water each morning, swam in the warm Caribbean, and let the girls run wild between the beach and the forest trails. Hammocks, fresh fruit, and not much of a schedule - exactly what we needed after the busy city.

Fri 27 Mar - Up to Bogota

From sea level to 2,600 meters! We flew up to the cool, mountain-ringed capital of Bogota. First stop was Cerro de Monserrate, riding up for sweeping views over the endless city (and the obligatory photo with the big BOGOTA letters). Then we dove into the chaos of Paloquemao market, where our guide had us tasting wild Colombian fruits we'd never seen before - lulo, mangostino, guanabana and more. The girls capped the day with a session at a local bouldering gym, scaling the colorful walls before a well-earned dinner.

Sat 28 Mar - Gardens, a Last Great Meal and Home

For our final day we wandered the Jardin Botanico de Bogota, ducking through greenhouses and rose gardens in the morning sun. We treated ourselves to one last beautiful Bogota lunch - elegant plates that were almost too pretty to eat - before heading to the airport. Adios, Colombia! From the walls of Cartagena to the island beaches to the heights of the Andes, what a trip. The girls are already asking when we can come back.
